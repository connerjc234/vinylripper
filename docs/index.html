Features

Everything you need to digitize vinyl

A focused toolkit that turns your turntable into a high-quality digital recording studio.

Live Recording

Record directly from your sound card with real-time waveform display. Supports any input device at any sample rate. Watch your recording come to life as you play.

Discogs Metadata

Look up artist, title, year, label, tracklist, and cover art. Tags your files automatically.

Waveform Editor

Drag split markers, undo/redo (Ctrl+Z/Y), zoom and pan. Minimap overview keeps you oriented.

Silence Detection

Auto-detects gaps between tracks. Adjustable sensitivity and minimum gap for perfect splits.

Vinyl Numbering

Automatic A1/A2/B1/B2 positions from Discogs data. Sub-track support for medleys (A1.a, A1.b).

Multi-Format Export

Save as FLAC (0-8 compression), MP3 (VBR 0-9), or AIFF (16/24/32-bit). Embedded metadata and cover art included. Choose lossless archival or space-efficient — your call.

Audio Restoration

Highpass rumble filter (30Hz) and declicking via FFmpeg. Clean up pops and rumble before export.

Persistent Settings

JSON config at ~/.config/vinylripper with env var overrides. Everything remembered between sessions.

How It Works

Vinyl to files in four steps

Connect your turntable, search for your album, record, and save. No audio engineering degree required.

1

Connect and Configure

Plug your USB turntable or audio interface into your computer. Select the input device from the dropdown. VinylRipper detects available hardware automatically.

2

Search Discogs

When you press Record, a search dialog opens. Search for your album on Discogs by artist and title. Select the matching release to pull in track names, year, and cover art.

3

Record Your Vinyl

Hit Record and start playing your record. The waveform updates in real time. When the side is done, press Stop.

4

Split and Save

Review the waveform, adjust split markers with drag-and-drop, and save individual tracks as FLAC, MP3, or AIFF files with full metadata and cover art embedded.
